# WIYS Published Article Examples

Reference list of actual published articles for calibrating the news scraper's
filtering and scoring system. These represent what "good content" looks like for WIYS.

## Content Categories (observed from ~500+ published articles)

### 1. State Laws & Policy (~25-30%)
- California Will Let You Return a Used Car Within 3 Days Starting in 2026
- Minnesota Workers Get Guaranteed 15-Minute Breaks Starting in 2026
- Hawaii Blocks Trump Order to Allow Fishing in Pacific Marine Monument
- Texas Just Made Squatting a Criminal Offense
- Colorado Bison Can No Longer Be Shot on Sight After 128 Years
- Florida Will Publicly Name Animal Abusers Starting January 2026
- Indiana Becomes First State to Ban Candy and Soda From Food Stamps
- Oregon Quietly Passed One of the Biggest Tribal Rights Laws in Decades

### 2. Hidden Gem Travel Features (~25%)
- This Wisconsin Peninsula Has 300 Miles of Coastline and Most Americans Have Never Heard of It
- Utah's Quietest National Park Has 2,000 Fruit Trees and Zero Traffic Lights
- Georgia's Secret Beach Island Has Ancient Oaks, Colonial Forts, and No Tourists
- This 'Lonely' Nevada Highway Has Ancient Forests, Hot Springs & a Darn Good Burger
- This Tiny Montana Town Just Beat Japan and Italy as the World's Top Travel Destination
- Spokane Has a Waterfall Downtown and Costs 35% Less Than Seattle
- South Dakota's Wildest Cave Holds 95% of a Formation Found Nowhere Else on Earth

### 3. Federal Policy Impact (~15%)
- Social Security Will Cut Benefits by 24% in 2034 Unless Congress Acts
- Medicare Part B Premiums Cross $200 for the First Time in 2026
- 22 States Are Raising Minimum Wages While 20 Stay Frozen at $7.25
- REAL ID rules and changes
- Tariff impacts on consumer prices
- ACA/Obamacare enrollment changes

### 4. National Parks & Public Lands (~10%)
- Feds Unlock 87,000 Acres of Hunting and Fishing Land Across 11 States
- US National Parks Win $3 Billion Fight, Congress Defies Trump
- RVs Now Banned in Zion National Park's Most Famous Road
- Colorado Opens North America's Largest Wildlife Crossing
- 13 Everyday Activities That Are Actually Illegal in US National Parks

### 5. Historical (~10%)
- Inside the Lost Native American Empire That Ruled Ancient Illinois for 300 Years
- Six Confederate Veterans Started a Social Club. It Became America's First Terror Group
- 90 Years Ago, the Government Asked Hunters to Buy a Stamp – It's Now Saved 6 Million Acres
- George Washington Was Already Pro-Vaccine 248 Years Ago

### 6. Economic / Cost of Living (~5%)
- America's Most Affordable Housing Market Is Hiding in Mississippi
- Oklahoma Beats Mississippi and Arkansas as America's Cheapest State
- Topeka Will Pay You $15,000 to Move There and Buy a House
- Building a Home in 2026 Could Cost You More Than Ever Before

### 7. Quirky / Fascinating (~5%)
- Arizona's 'Stupid Motorist Law' Is Still the Best-Named Statute in America
- You Can Go to Prison for 25 Years for Cutting Down a Cactus in Arizona
- Oklahoma Lawmaker Wanted Roosters to Fight Robots Instead of Each Other
- Lawyer Sues IRS to Claim Her Golden Retriever as a Tax Dependent

## Common Headline Patterns
- "This [State] [Place]..." — most common travel feature format
- "[State] Just [action]..." — new law/policy articles
- "[Number] States Are..." — multi-state policy roundups
- "America's [superlative]..." — national-level stories
- "You Can [activity] at This [place]..." — destination features
- "[Place] Is/Has [surprising fact]..." — hidden gem reveals

## Nuances for Filtering
- Political content IS covered when it's policy impact (not horse-race)
- Trump administration actions ARE covered factually
- ICE/immigration IS covered as policy impact
- Casino/Vegas IS covered as tourism news (not gambling promotion)
- Commercial businesses ARE included if historically/culturally significant
- Cost/affordability IS a major angle
- Relocation incentive stories ARE covered ("X will pay you to move there")
